Oh, Mother Russia...

Russia is a giant country with a massive cultural heritage, which spans through the centuries. Just look at influential Russian composers, such as M. Glinka, P. Tchaikovsky, M. Mussorgsky and I. Stravinsky - they are known worldwide and are praised by musicologists, with their music continuing to be studied today. Russian cinema is also extremely influential with amazing films created by directors such as A. Tarkovsky, S. Eisenstein and A. Balabanov (one of my favourite filmographers of all time). Don’t get me going on the literature, Russian is such a complex and broad language - it’s a poet's dream, trust me.

This historical melting pot of musical, visual and literately talent has inevitably climaxed in today’s Russian music industry, where music videos are worth films, poetry can be turned into books and music takes you to a different dimension, where sound paints pictures and leaves you begging for more.

Today I will be your guide on Russian more experimental musical output and introduce you to my top 4 artists, who are so unique that you will have a hard time finding something similar anywhere else.

4 Positions of Bruno

4 Позиции Бруно is one of those bands that has left mesmerised for many years and I just can’t help but be a fanboy of their music. The act was founded in 2002 and consists of two people - Alexander Sitnikov and Anton Klevtsov in Ekaterinburg, which was later expanded by multiple side projects, such as Ptitsu Em’ (Птицу Емъ), Ty23 (Ты23), Porez Na Sobake (Порез на собаке) and others, adding to their ginormous discography of 30+ releases. They jokingly describe their music as “Toxic Dub” and it is your best attempt to possibly categorise their releases. But putting them into some sort of a frame would be a grave mistake - you cannot and you should not do this. Their influences are extremely vast - ranging from ambient and illbient to dub, hip hop and chanson. Talking about the lyrical context of their songs is a tough task as well, one song will present a tale of a cursed train ride, where every passenger will certainly die, while the other one gives you a glimpse of what it is like to be a nonconformist ghost, while spicing it up with some silly jokes and a perfect usage of slang words.

4 Позиции Бруно masterfully takes you on a psychedelic conceptual journey, driving you through the roads built by your preconscious and unconscious or emerging you within the grim and mystical worlds carefully crafted through the use of poetry and sound design. If I would describe those guys in a few words, they would be: “surreal mystical fairy tales based on unreal experiences”.

I strongly urge you to check this band out ASAP, as it is one of those unique acts that can only exist here and now.

Shortparis

A Saint Petersburg band Shortparis is what punk would be if the members of the band would work in an art museum (The singer Nikolai Komyagin used to give tours at the St. Petersburg's Museum of Art). They are loud, experimental, erotic and chaotic. The act’s music yet again cannot be categorised - they combine political topics in their lyrics with hard-hitting percussion, esoteric singing, chromatic synth lines and screeches of electric guitars while spicing it up with theatre-like performances, resulting in something truly artistic. After seeing them play in Manchester, I was wowed by their presence and musicianship. 

While producing outstanding music, Shorparis’ music videos are incredibly beautiful and sophisticated as well. It is a common trend amongst Russian acts - to create such high-quality videos, that one may ask themselves - how on earth did these guys manage to put so many talented people in one place and create something so remarkable with such little budget (maybe not right now as Shortparis are signed to UMG Russia, but still keep in mind, while being quite popular, these are still underground artists who are just pushing the envelope). Just have a look and listen for yourself.

Husky

Хаски is a Russian rapper who is beloved by many but certainly not the government. His performances were banned multiple times by the local authorities due to his politically-charged lyrics, which actions enraged many of his fans and Russian entertainment community as a whole. Nonetheless, he is mor popular than ever. The closest analogy would be Childish Gambino or Tricky, but still Хаски is a completely different beast and is a phenomenon. His music is dark, gritty and unapologetically truthful with all of his videos scoring more than millions of views each, with no support from any major labels (at least to my research).

Talking about the videos, yet again, this is Hollywood material thanks to Lado Kvataniya, which is Husky's go to director in recent times. Videos to songs like "Убей меня", "Бесконечный Магазин" and "Реванш" create such a vibe and atmosphere, that they the frames from each clip will stay in your memory forever. I just cannot stress enough, what a melting pot of talent these productions are. And all of this just for the sake of creating outstanding works of art, nothing else.

Aigel

Аигел have become reached mainstream popularity with their hit song "Татарин", which story told about a woman, whose just freed convict boyfriend was returning home. Unfortunately for Аигел, it has gathered a lot of attention from people, who are far way from the underground electronic music of the duo. But nevertheless, Aigel Guysina and Ilya Baramiya are still extremely successful and have just recently finished a soundtrack to a TV series called "Топи". By the way, Ilya Baramiya was a quite known music producer before the project, with such acts as IDM inspired "EU", "2H Company", as well as "Самое большое простое число".

The music of Аигел could be described as elctrorap comprised of electronic beats of Ilya and Russian/ Tatar lyrics and singing by Aigel herself. The topics are vast and all of the songs offer something different and unique, be it sounds or magnificently unusual vocal performances.

Oh, and by the way, have I not yet mentioned the quality of the music videos? The "You Are Born" video has received a silver reward of Cannes Lions International Festival 2020. To be honest, I am not surprised, but yet I am extremely proud.
